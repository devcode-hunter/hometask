Revolut - IT Administrator Home Task

My laptop ain't as fast as it used to be! :(

1. Restart Your Mac

  • Restarting can clear temporary issues and free up system resources.
  • Click the Apple logo in the top-left corner, select Restart, and wait for the system to reboot.

2. Close Unnecessary Programs

  • 1) Press Command + Option + Esc to open the Force Quit Applications window.
  • 2) Force quit any applications that are not responding or that you do not need.
  • 3) If you're a developer/designer and you run resource-intensive programs, ensure your device's specifications can hold the ground.

3. Free Up Disk Space

  • Go to the Apple logo > About This Mac > Storage.
  • Click on Manage to delete unnecessary files and uninstall unused applications.

4. Update macOS to Sonoma!

  • Go to the Apple logo > System Settings > Software Update.
  • 2) Check for updates and install any available.

5. Check your RAM!

  • 1) Is your RAM too low? Sadly, you can't upgrade the RAM on your Mac :(
  • 2) If you're a developer/designer and you run resource-intensive programs, ensure your device's specifications can hold the ground.

6. Disable Startup Programs

  • 1) Go to the Apple logo > System Settings > Users & Groups.
  • 2) Select your user account, then click on Login Items and remove unnecessary startup programs.
  • 3) Go to the Startup tab and disable unnecessary startup programs.

7. Monitor Activity (Something like Task Manager in Windows!)

  • Open Activity Monitor from the Utilities folder in Applications.
  • Check for applications using a high amount of CPU or memory and quit them if necessary.
