Monitor not displaying? Read on!

1. Check your cable!

  • 1) Ensure all cables (power, HDMI, DisplayPort, Thunderbolt, etc.) are securely connected. Needs a snug fit!
  • 2) Try using a different cable. The current one could be faulty! If you can't source for another cable, we have some spares!

2. Do a power cycle!

  • 1) Turn off both your Mac and the monitor.
  • 2) Unplug them from the power source.
  • 3) Have a coffee, then plug them back in and turn them on! :)

3. It could be your display settings!

  • 1) On your Mac, head to the Apple menu and go to System Settings > Displays in the sidebar.
  • 2) Click on Detect Displays to see if the Mac can find the external monitor. (You may also want to hold on the Option key!)

4. Resolution and Refresh Rate

  • 1) Ensure the monitorâ€™s resolution and refresh rate are compatible with your Mac. Generally if you're using a newer monitor, it shouldn't be an issue.
  • 2) You can change these settings in System Settings > Displays.

5. Mirror or Extend Display

  • 1) Go to System Settings > Displays and check if the display is set to Mirror or Extend.
  • 1) Try switching between these settings and see if it works!

6. Updated to latest OS?

  • 1) Worth giving it a try! Ensure your Mac is running the latest version of macOS (you should ideally be on Sonoma now! Go to System Settings > Software Update.

7. Reset NVRAM/PRAM (Only if you're on Intel Macs!)

  • 1) Shut down your Mac.
  • 2) Turn it on and immediately press and hold Option + Command + P + R for about 20 seconds. This will reset the NVRAM/PRAM.

8. Reset SMC (Only if you're on Intel Macs!)

    • 1) Shut down your Mac.
    • 2) Press and hold Shift + Control + Option and the power button for 10 seconds, then release all keys and turn on your Mac.

9. Test with a buddy's monitor or another Mac!

  • 1) Connect your Mac to another monitor to see if the issue persists.
  • 2) Alternatively, connect a different Mac to your monitor to determine if the problem is with the monitor or the Mac.
