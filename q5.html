What is that weird icon? I can't boot up!

1. Restart Your Mac

  • 1) Press and hold the power button to turn off your Mac.
  • 2) Wait a few seconds, then press the power button again to turn it on.

2. Check Startup Disk

  • 1) Press and hold the Option key immediately after turning on your Mac.
  • 2) Select your startup disk from the list of available disks (usually the main Disk should be labeled as Macintosh HD).

3. Reset NVRAM/PRAM (Only for Intel-based Macs)

  • 1) Shut down your Mac.
  • 2) Turn it on and immediately press and hold Option + Command + P + R for about 20 seconds.
  • 3) Release the keys and let your Mac continue to start up normally.
  • 4) If you're using M1, M2 or M3 Macs, the above doesn't apply. Move on to the next steps!

4. Use Disk Utility in macOS Recovery

  • 1) Turn off your Mac.
  • 2) Turn it on and immediately press and hold Command + R until you see the Apple logo or a spinning globe (Intel-based Macs). If you're using M1, M2 or M3, press and hold the Power Button until you see a message that says 'Continue to Hold for Startup Options'.
  • 3) Select Disk Utility, then click Continue.
  • 4) Select your startup disk and click First Aid to check for and repair disk errors.

5. Reinstall macOS

  • 1) If Disk Utility finds no errors or repairs the disk but the problem persists, you may need to reinstall macOS.
  • 2) In macOS Recovery, select Reinstall macOS and follow the on-screen instructions.
